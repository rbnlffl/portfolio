About Me

My name is Robin Löffel and I'm 20 years old. I live in the wonderful country of alps, Switzerland. But well, to be honest, not really close to them. Bern is the city my heart chose and where I work and go to school. Since I can remember, I have had an interest in anything aesthetically pleasing. Architecture, fashion, photography, nature – you name it.

My career started at the sweet age of 16, when I got an apprenticeship at the Swiss Government as a Software Engineer. During this time I discovered that I like not to just look at beautiful things, but that I also want to make them. I got into a team which was focussing on Java web applications, where I could take over the frontend tasks.

After finishing my apprenticeship and getting my diploma, digital design started to get more and more of my attention. So I started my study to become an Interaction Designer at the School of Visual Arts Bern in late summer 2015. And that's where I am now, while still working at the Swiss Government. In a new team full of Frontend Developers and UX Designers.

What I Do

I focus on web development and design. This includes crafting mockups, developing simple backends, usability testing, user interface design, user experience design, image editing/optimization, typography and CMS integration. All together in a neat package.

I mainly code in HTML, CSS (LESS, SASS), JavaScript (jQuery, AngularJS, Gulp) and sometimes PHP. Amongst others, I'm familiar with WordPress, Grav, Photoshop, Lightroom, Pixelmator and Sketch.
